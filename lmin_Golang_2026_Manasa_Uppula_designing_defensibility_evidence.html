Designing for Defensibility: Evidence-Centric Compliance Architecture in Customer-Facing Financial Platforms

Video size:

Abstract

Customer-facing financial web platforms operate under persistent regulatory scrutiny, continuous audit obligations, and a constant expectation of dispute-readiness. In these environments, compliance cannot be deferred to documentation or treated as a post-implementation concern — it must be deliberately engineered into the platform architecture from day one. This session introduces an evidence-centric compliance architecture approach in which regulatory obligations are translated into explicit architectural constraints that shape interaction flows, system boundaries, and operational controls from the ground up. The presentation examines how platforms can be designed to consistently produce structured, verifiable evidence of critical customer interactions — capturing what information was presented, how acknowledgement or consent was obtained, and how system decisions were derived. By embedding evidence generation directly into interaction workflows, platforms remain fully explainable during audits, regulatory inquiries, or disputes — without depending on manual reconstruction or assumptions after the fact. The session further explores auditability and traceability as first-class architectural qualities. Auditability enables confident, reliable reconstruction of critical events; traceability establishes clear links between regulatory expectations, specific design controls, and observable system behaviour. Together, these capabilities reduce interpretive ambiguity and support predictable compliance outcomes as platforms scale and evolve. The talk concludes by addressing governance and change-control practices that preserve compliance integrity over time. By identifying compliance-critical components early and applying risk-based review and release strategies, teams can modernise and innovate without eroding existing controls.
