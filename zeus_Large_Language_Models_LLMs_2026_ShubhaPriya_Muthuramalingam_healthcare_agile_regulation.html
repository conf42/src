Agile Transformation for Scalable Healthcare IT in Regulated AI Era

Video size:

Abstract

Healthcare organizations are investing heavily in large-scale IT modernization to support electronic health records, interoperability, analytics, and increasingly AI- and LLM-driven clinical decision support. Yet many initiatives struggle under traditional waterfall models that depend on long, sequential phases and assume stable requirements. In dynamic healthcare environmentswhere clinical workflows, regulatory policies, and operational priorities continuously evolvethis rigidity often results in delayed delivery, misaligned solutions, and increased risk. This session explores how Agile transformation enables healthcare organizations to deliver complex enterprise IT programs more effectively in the age of AI and LLMs. By shifting from multi-year delivery cycles to short, iterative sprints of 24 weeks, teams can deliver incremental value while incorporating continuous feedback from clinicians, administrators, and compliance stakeholders. Drawing from large healthcare IT programs that integrate legacy systems, modern platforms, regulatory constraints, and cross-disciplinary teams, the session highlights how Agile practices enhance collaboration across clinicians, developers, data analysts, and compliance experts. This approach enables early validation of workflows such as patient intake, care management, and AI-assisted decision support, reducing design and implementation risks. The talk also presents a scalable Agile architecture tailored for regulated healthcare environments, spanning experience, application, integration, data, and infrastructure layers. This model supports incremental delivery of capabilitiessuch as provider portals, interoperability APIs, and AI-enabled systemswhile maintaining stability and compliance.
