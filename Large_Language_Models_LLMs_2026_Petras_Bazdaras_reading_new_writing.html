Reading is the New Writing: Why the AI Era Demands Absolute Mastery of the Basics

Video size:

Abstract

In 2026, Large Language Models have commoditized raw code syntax, transforming hours of boilerplate writing into seconds of prompt execution. However, this rapid generation has introduced a dangerous industry-wide delusion: mistaking “compiled and working” for “good engineering.” As software engineers shift from code typists to system reviewers, reading and critically auditing code has officially become the primary engineering act. This talk explores why the explosion of AI-generated output demands an absolute, uncompromising return to Computer Science fundamentals. Attendees will discover the dangers of cognitive muscle atrophy, confront the production-born “Autopsy Problem,” and learn how to transition from a passive consumer of AI output to a rigorous “Managing Editor” capable of securing and scaling modern software architectures.
