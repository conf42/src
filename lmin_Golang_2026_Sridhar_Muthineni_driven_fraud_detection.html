AI-Driven Fraud Detection & Personalization in Scalable Go Fintech Systems

Video size:

Abstract

The rapid growth of mobile fintech applications has reshaped digital banking, increasing both transaction volumes and exposure to sophisticated fraud such as account takeovers, synthetic identities, and money laundering. At the same time, users expect highly personalized financial experiences. This session presents a unified AI-driven approach to fraud prevention and personalization, tailored for high-performance systems built with Go. We explore modern machine learning architectures—including gradient-boosted decision trees, RNNs, graph neural networks, and transformer-based models—for real-time anomaly detection across high-frequency transaction streams. Special focus is given to how Go’s concurrency model, low-latency execution, and efficient memory management enable scalable inference pipelines and streaming architectures. Key performance metrics such as detection accuracy, false-positive rates, and inference latency are examined, highlighting trade-offs in production fintech systems. On the personalization side, we cover recommendation systems, reinforcement learning, and LLM-powered conversational agents that enable adaptive credit scoring, behavior-aware insights, and tailored product offerings. These systems are evaluated based on relevance, engagement, and user trust. We also address real-world challenges including class imbalance, concept drift, explainability under regulations (GDPR, PSD2, EU AI Act), and fairness in lending. Privacy-preserving techniques such as federated learning, differential privacy, and on-device inference are discussed in the context of secure Go-based deployments. Through industry case studies, attendees will see how AI systems improve fraud detection while enhancing customer experience. We conclude with emerging challenges in adversarial robustness, real-time graph analytics, and responsible AI adoption in financial systems.
