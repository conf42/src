Building Voice AI for People Who Don’t Trust AI

Video size:

Abstract

Most AI products assume users are comfortable installing apps, creating accounts, and talking to chatbots. Many people are not.

In this talk, a founding engineer building production voice-first AI systems explores what happens when you remove the app entirely and let users interact with an LLM agent through a normal phone call.

The session focuses on designing conversational AI for audiences often ignored by mainstream AI products: people who are skeptical of AI, uncomfortable with modern apps, or simply unwilling to learn another interface.

Rather than treating the model itself as the main challenge, the talk explores the harder problem of building trust. Pavel shares practical lessons from deploying real-world voice agents, including conversation design, failure handling, latency expectations, speech interaction patterns, and why familiar interfaces like phone calls can dramatically lower adoption barriers.

Attendees will leave with a grounded perspective on voice AI systems, along with practical ideas for building AI products that feel more accessible, trustworthy, and human for everyday users.
