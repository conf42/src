Compliance-by-Construction for Scalable, Privacy-Resilient Credit Apps

Video size:

Abstract

Co-branded credit card ecosystems operate at the intersection of regulated banking infrastructure and high-volume consumer platforms, where security breaches are common and financial impact is significant. This session presents a compliance-by-construction architecture that embeds regulatory, security, and privacy controls directly into system design while sustaining large-scale transaction processing. Grounded in economic security modeling, the framework aligns security investments with breach probability, enabling organizations to optimize spending relative to risk exposure. Behavioral compliance research further strengthens governance by incorporating empirically validated predictors of policy adherence, emphasizing the combined role of organizational culture and technical enforcement. From a systems perspective, the architecture leverages event-driven microservices and advanced logging to handle high-volume, real-time telemetryaddressing the reality that many failures leave no trace in traditional logs. PCI-DSS-aligned micro-segmentation and AI-assisted tokenization techniques reduce computational and memory overhead while preserving strong security and user experience outcomes. At scale, high-performance networking and distributed transaction protocols support extremely high throughput with microsecond-level latency. These capabilities enable real-time underwriting, fraud detection, and partner integrations without compromising compliance or resilience. The session also explores how AI/LLM-driven components can enhance anomaly detection, observability, and adaptive policy enforcement in regulated environments.
