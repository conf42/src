Shipping AI Features That Don’t Break in Production

Video size:

Abstract

LLM demos are easy. Shipping AI features that real users can depend on is where the hard engineering starts.

In this talk, an Android engineer working on AI-driven mobile features at Revolut shares lessons from building production AI products at scale, alongside developing an independent AI coaching app for padel players.

The session focuses on the messy reality behind AI integrations in mobile apps: latency, inference costs, hallucinations, unreliable outputs, rate limits, and the challenge of designing UX around systems that are fundamentally non-deterministic.

Instead of focusing on ideal demo flows, the talk explores what happens when the model gives incomplete, wrong, delayed, or unusable responses, and how engineering teams can build proper safeguards, fallbacks, and user experiences around those failures.

Attendees will leave with practical patterns, architectural decisions, and product lessons for making LLM-powered mobile features more stable, predictable, and usable in production environments.
