Building Reliable LLM Systems: Managing Decision Latency and Cognitive Load in the AI Era

Video size:

Abstract

Large Language Models are accelerating every phase of software delivery from code generation to documentation, testing, and analysis. Yet as execution speeds increase, a new constraint emerges: human decision capacity. In LLM-enabled enterprises, reliability and governance are increasingly limited not by model capability, but by decision latency and cognitive overload. This talk reframes these human constraints as first-class system properties in AI platform design. Decision latency appears in model approvals, prompt reviews, data governance checks, safety validations, and incident response loops. When experimentation is fast but ownership and accountability are unclear, AI initiatives accumulate hidden risk and stalled progress. Cognitive load intensifies as engineers manage model selection, evaluation metrics, hallucination mitigation, prompt engineering, security boundaries, and compliance requirements. As AI increases change frequency, unmanaged complexity leads to burnout and fragile systems. Using a distributed-systems lens, this session introduces patterns such as explicit decision boundaries, clear AI platform ownership models, policy-as-code guardrails, and opinionated golden paths for LLM adoption. Attendees will gain practical strategies to convert AI acceleration into sustainable reliability, responsible governance, and high-performing AI engineering teams.
