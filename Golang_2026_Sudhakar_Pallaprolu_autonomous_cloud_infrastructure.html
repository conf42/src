Autonomous Cloud Infrastructure with Go: Building Self-Healing Systems

Video size:

Abstract

Enterprises are losing an average of dollars per minute due to unplanned IT downtime (Gartner), highlighting the urgent need for resilient, self-healing infrastructure. At Conf42 Golang 2026, this session explores how Go-powered cloud platforms are enabling the rise of the autonomous enterprise through intelligent, self-managing systems. We’ll examine how AIOps adoption has accelerated in recent years, allowing teams to automate incident detection, root-cause analysis, and remediation using machine learning and predictive analytics. With real-time anomaly detection across logs and metrics, AI-driven models are helping reduce mean time to detect and mean time to resolve, strengthening reliability in distributed Go-based microservices environments. The talk will unpack practical use cases where predictive failure prevention minimized outages and how policy-driven orchestration frameworks evolved from simple provisioning to closed-loop automation—forming the backbone of resilient cloud-native systems. We’ll also explore the next phase of Infrastructure-as-Code, where intelligent configurations dynamically adapt to operational context. Through platforms such as Azure Automanage, AWS Fault Injection Simulator, and GCP Active Assist, organizations are automating remediation and chaos validation, enabling engineering teams to focus on innovation. Attendees will gain practical architectural patterns, tooling strategies, and cultural insights needed to design and operate autonomous, self-healing cloud systems using Go.
