Building Real-Time AI Decision Systems with Large Language Models

Video size:

Abstract

As enterprises adopt large language models across business and engineering workflows, the challenge is no longer just deploying AI models, but integrating them into reliable, real-time decision systems. This session explores how organizations are combining LLMs, analytics, and modern data platforms to support scalable operational intelligence across complex environments. The talk examines practical implementation challenges including fragmented data sources, governance concerns, infrastructure scalability, and maintaining trust in AI-assisted recommendations. It also discusses how enterprises are moving from static reporting and isolated AI experiments toward continuously evolving systems that combine predictive analytics, streaming data, and LLM-assisted insights. Drawing from enterprise implementation studies and modernization initiatives, the session will cover architectural approaches for integrating AI models into production workflows while balancing performance, observability, and operational reliability. Additional discussion will focus on how reinforcement learning, retrieval-based systems, and real-time analytics pipelines are being applied to improve responsiveness and decision quality without adding unnecessary system complexity. This session is intended for AI engineers, software developers, architects, platform teams, and technology leaders interested in practical approaches to building scalable and reliable AI-driven decision systems.
