From Data Pipelines to AI Products: Operationalizing Machine Learning and Generative AI in Enterprise Decision Platforms

Video size:

Abstract

Modern enterprises are rapidly evolving from isolated analytics initiatives to fully operational AI-driven decision platforms, but scaling these systems reliably remains a significant challenge. This session explores how organizations can bridge the gap between data engineering, MLOps, and AI productization to deliver production-grade intelligence at scale. Drawing on real-world implementations across healthcare, marketing analytics, and technology startups, we examine how robust data pipelines, versioned datasets, and automated deployment workflows enable faster time-to-market and improved operational efficiency. The talk highlights how Database DevOps practices such as continuous integration for data, schema evolution management, and pipeline observability form the backbone of scalable AI systems. We dive into architectures that combine machine learning and generative AI, including large language models and retrieval augmented generation, with a strong focus on reliability, reproducibility, and governance. Attendees will learn how modern data platforms support continuous model experimentation, real-time inference, and seamless integration with transactional and analytical systems. Key areas include designing resilient cloud native data and machine learning pipelines, implementing MLOps practices for faster and safer model deployments, ensuring data quality and governance in AI workflows, enabling observability across data and model layers, and scaling AI systems for real time decision making. This session emphasizes a practical DevOps first approach to AI, where data, models, and infrastructure are treated as versioned, testable, and deployable assets. By aligning engineering discipline with AI innovation, organizations can move beyond experimentation to build reliable and scalable AI products that deliver measurable business impact.
