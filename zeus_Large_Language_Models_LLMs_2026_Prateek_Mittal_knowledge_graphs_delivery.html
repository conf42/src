Shipping with Context: Knowledge Graphs as the Backbone of AI-First Software Delivery

Video size:

Abstract

AI is changing how software gets written, but writing code faster is only part of the challenge. The harder question is whether AI can understand what that code means inside the context of an organization: the services it touches, the risks it introduces, the teams responsible for it, the policies it must follow, and the production systems it may impact.

In this talk, we’ll explore why knowledge graphs are becoming a foundational layer for AI-first software delivery. As engineering environments become more complex, critical context is often fragmented across CI/CD pipelines, security tools, observability platforms, cloud infrastructure, ticketing systems, and team workflows. Without that connected context, AI agents can generate outputs, but they struggle to make reliable, governed decisions.

We’ll discuss how knowledge graphs differ from traditional observability platforms, how they help AI agents reason across the software delivery lifecycle, and why shared context is becoming essential for DevOps, SecOps, FinOps, and platform engineering. We’ll also cover the role of RBAC, policy guardrails, and governance in making enterprise AI systems trustworthy.

The next bottleneck in AI adoption will not be model capability. It will be context.
