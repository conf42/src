Pod Security Policy is Dead, Long Live...?

Video size:

Abstract

What are Pod Security Policies? What do you mean, they are deprecated? What am I going to do?!

Summary

  • Who's worked with Kubernetes pods before? Anyone? Hey, in the right room. Pods are the smallest deployable units of computing that you can create and manage. They represent like a single instance of an application running in your cluster.
  • PSPs give cluster admins an ability to impose some limits on the things that are running in the cluster. They were deprecated in 1.21 last year, and will be removed entirely in August this year. Here's an example of what you can do with a vanilla debian image.
  • psps fundamentally sound like a great idea, but there's a whole heap of usability issues. The policy is based on the user that created the pod, not the workload. You can be one bad pod away from losing absolutely everything that you own. What are the alternatives?
  • Using a cluster enforced policy does not guarantee any real security. You may well find that the cure is actually worse than the disease. The good news is there are answers. They are simple, but not necessarily easy.
