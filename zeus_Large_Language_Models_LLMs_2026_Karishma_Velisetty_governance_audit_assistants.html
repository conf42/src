From Acceleration to Assurance: Governing AI Code Assistants in SOX and ICFR Audit Analytics

Video size:

Abstract

AI code assistants powered by large language models are rapidly reshaping how audit analytics tools are built, enabling teams to generate SQL queries, Python scripts, and testing routines in seconds. While these capabilities promise significant productivity gains, they also introduce critical risks that challenge the integrity of SOX and ICFR compliance processes. This session examines the dual reality of AI-assisted audit development through evidence-based insights. On one hand, AI can efficiently scaffold common audit proceduressuch as data extraction, Benfords Law analysis, and duplicate payment detectionreducing development time and lowering technical barriers. On the other hand, these gains diminish when tasks require deep accounting logic or complex reasoning, such as ASC 606 revenue recognition or multi-entity reconciliation workflows. More importantly, security and compliance risks are often underestimated. Studies show that a substantial portion of AI-generated code contains vulnerabilitiesincluding SQL injection risks, hard-coded credentials, and insecure data handlingdirectly threatening the reliability of audit evidence. Compounding the issue, developers frequently exhibit increased confidence in AI-generated outputs despite reduced security quality. To address this gap, the session introduces a practical governance framework tailored for compliance-driven environments. This includes integrating automated security scanning (Bandit, SonarQube, CodeQL) into CI/CD pipelines, establishing controlled prompt engineering standards for secure and compliant code generation, and applying test-driven development practices to validate AI-generated outputs before they are accepted as audit evidence. Attendees will gain a clear, actionable approach to leveraging LLM-powered development while preserving the rigor, traceability, and assurance standards required for SOX and ICFR testing.
