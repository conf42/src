Digital PMOs in Retail with Jira and Confluence: Governance Through Transparency

Video size:

Abstract

Retail technology portfolios that span e commerce, supply chain, and in store platforms often struggle under traditional PMO models. Status tracking is frequently fragmented across spreadsheets and disconnected tools, making it difficult to maintain a single source of truth, identify dependencies early, or apply consistent governance controls. These gaps slow decision making, reduce transparency, and create operational inefficiencies across the portfolio. This session presents a Digital PMO framework that integrates Jira and Confluence to establish a unified governance and execution environment. The framework translates established PMO and IT governance concepts into a practical operating model built on structured workflows, standardized templates, and integrated documentation. It connects strategic themes, initiatives, OKRs, epics, delivery telemetry, risk signals, and executive reporting within a single system of record that supports both engineering teams and leadership. A retail case study demonstrates the framework’s application within an Automotive Care Center technology portfolio that involved more than 20 cross functional teams and approximately 50 initiatives per quarter over a six month period. Quantitative results included a 30 percent reduction in PMO operational effort within six months and an additional 30 percent reduction at 12 months, while program manager roles were reduced from eight to three. Stakeholders also reported improved transparency, faster trade off decisions, and greater trust in portfolio reporting. Attendees will learn how integrated Jira and Confluence environments can support scalable governance, real time portfolio visibility, and more efficient program management in complex retail technology ecosystems.
