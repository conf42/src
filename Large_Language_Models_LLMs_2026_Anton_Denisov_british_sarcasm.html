Teaching and LLM to Catch British Sarcasm

Video size:

Abstract

Customer dissatisfaction is often expressed indirectly, especially in professional communication where complaints are softened by politeness, understatement, or even silence.

This talk covers the design and deployment of a sentiment analysis system that processes emails and phone conversations to identify dissatisfaction and churn risk. We’ll explore the challenges of working with real-world communication, including ambiguous language, British politeness, incomplete data, and channel-specific behaviors.

The session focuses on dataset design, labeling, prompt engineering, LLM-based classification, and the lessons learned from running the system in production.
