Shift-Left Performance Engineering for RAG and LLM Platforms with CI/CD Performance Signatures

Video size:

Abstract

As organizations adopt large language model (LLM) and retrieval-augmented generation workloads in production, performance engineering can no longer remain a late-stage validation activity. LLM-enabled systems introduce unique challenges, including latency variability, multi-service dependency chains, infrastructure elasticity, and the difficulty of establishing objective release readiness criteria. In these environments, defects discovered late in the lifecycle are costly, slow to diagnose, and disruptive to delivery velocity. This session presents a practical shift-left performance engineering framework for modern AI and cloud-native platforms, integrating Apache JMeter for workload simulation, Jenkins for CI/CD orchestration, and Dynatrace and Grafana for full-stack observability. At the center of the approach is Performance Signature Analysis, a statistical baseline comparison method that automates pass/fail quality gates and removes subjective interpretation from regression detection. Applied consistently across code changes, this method enables teams to validate system behavior within minutes and detect degradations before they reach production. The talk focuses on real implementation patterns: self-service Jenkins pipeline templates for developers, automated quality gates for every commit, live Grafana dashboards for test visibility, and Dynatrace Davis AI for root-cause identification across application, API, database, and infrastructure layers. These practices are especially relevant for LLM and AI platforms, where performance regressions may emerge from orchestration logic, downstream services, vector retrieval layers, or infrastructure contention rather than model inference alone. A case study from an enterprise microservices environment demonstrates measurable outcomes, including reduced production performance incidents, faster response times, increased automated test frequency, and significantly improved mean time to detect. Attendees will leave with practical guidance for embedding repeatable, data-driven performance validation into CI/CD pipelines and building performance-first engineering practices for AI-era systems.
