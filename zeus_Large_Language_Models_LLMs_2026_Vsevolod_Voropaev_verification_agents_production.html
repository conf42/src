The Judge Problem: Why Your Agentic Verifier is Silently Rotting in Production

Video size:

Abstract

Every agentic system eventually faces the same question: who judges the agent? Most teams bolt on a verifier late, treat it as QA, and watch it collapse under real-world load. This session argues that the verifier is the hardest component to build and the most expensive to get wrong. Drawing on production work with a bandit-based verification layer routing across Claude, GPT, and Gemini, we will cover: The Intelligence Inversion: Why verifiers should usually be smarter than the agents they watch. Systematic Disagreement: How to identify and exploit predictable patterns in LLM-as-judge setups. Dynamic Routing: Moving beyond simple ensemble voting to sophisticated bandit routing. Scale Economics: What true verification costs look like when moving from demo to production. Attendees will leave with a clear framework for building verification layers that are more resilient than the agents they monitor.
