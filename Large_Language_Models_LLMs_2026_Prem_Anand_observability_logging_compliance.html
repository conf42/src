Log-Centric Intelligence: Enabling Real-Time Compliance Observability with LLM-Ready Architectures

Video size:

Abstract

Modern financial ecosystems operate across highly distributed environments where critical compliance data is fragmented among heterogeneous databases, messaging layers, and operational platforms. This fragmentation creates significant challenges for Anti-Money Laundering (AML), sanctions screening, and customer verification workflows that increasingly demand real-time visibility, explainability, and operational intelligence. Traditional reporting approaches depend heavily on cross-system database joins and centralized storage models, introducing latency, scalability limitations, and elevated security exposure. This session presents a practical architecture that transforms distributed logs into a unified operational intelligence layer capable of supporting both real-time compliance monitoring and emerging Large Language Model (LLM) integrations. Instead of treating logs as passive audit artifacts, the proposed design introduces a lightweight wrapper library and a standardized correlation identifier that enable asynchronous event streams from disparate systems to be stitched into a continuous, traceable narrative without requiring physical database consolidation. Attendees will learn how this decoupled architecture powers database-agnostic dashboards, accelerates anomaly detection, and simplifies observability across distributed financial systems. The talk will also explore how structured log intelligence creates an LLM-ready foundation for advanced operational use cases such as natural language compliance investigations, contextual alert summarization, intelligent incident triage, and conversational analytics. Through real-world architectural lessons, this session will discuss implementation tradeoffs, scalability considerations, security implications, and operational outcomes observed while building and evolving the system. Participants will leave with actionable insights into designing resilient, observable, and AI-compatible compliance infrastructures that reduce engineering friction while improving transparency, responsiveness, and decision-making across complex enterprise environments.
