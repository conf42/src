Modern Distributed Databases: Architecture, DevOps, and Operational Trade-offs

Video size:

Abstract

As organizations scale cloud-native platforms, traditional monolithic databases struggle to meet the demands of high availability, global distribution, and real-time data processing. This session explores the evolution of distributed database architectures and the critical role DevOps practices play in building, deploying, and operating resilient data platforms at scale. We will examine foundational concepts such as the CAP theorem, replication models, sharding strategies, and consensus protocols like Raft and Paxos that enable modern distributed databases to maintain availability, performance, and fault tolerance. The talk will also highlight how DevOps-driven automation through infrastructure as code, configuration management, and continuous deployment improves database reliability, scalability, and operational efficiency in production environments. Additionally, the session will address modern security and governance practices including zero-trust infrastructure, certificate-based authentication, and distributed key management. Attendees will gain practical insights into managing distributed databases through DevOps methodologies, optimizing operational workflows, and building secure, scalable, and highly automated data platforms for modern enterprise and cloud-native applications.
