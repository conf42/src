AI-Driven Database Tuning in DevOps: Real-World Lessons from Self-Optimizing Systems

Video size:

Abstract

Database performance tuning has traditionally relied on manual intervention, static rules, and reactive troubleshooting—approaches that often struggle to keep pace with modern DevOps environments. In this session, we share practical lessons from building and deploying AI-driven database optimization systems that continuously adapt to real-world workloads. Drawing from production case studies across finance, e-commerce, and healthcare, we explore how machine learning models were integrated into DevOps pipelines to automate indexing, query optimization, and configuration tuning. We’ll discuss what worked, what failed, and how iterative improvements led to measurable gains in performance, scalability, and operational efficiency. Attendees will gain insight into how these systems reduced manual DBA effort, improved query response times, and lowered infrastructure costs. The talk also covers architectural decisions, including how to safely deploy self-tuning mechanisms in live environments, ensure observability, and maintain control over automated changes. We will demonstrate an open-source toolkit that analyzes query logs in real time and evolves database configurations with minimal human intervention. This session provides actionable guidance for engineers and teams looking to integrate intelligent automation into their database DevOps workflows while avoiding common pitfalls.
