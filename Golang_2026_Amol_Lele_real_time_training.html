Real-Time ML Training Profiling in Go: Cut Costs by 50%

Video size:

Abstract

Machine learning training workloads now consume staggering resources, with training GPT-3 alone requiring 1,287 MWh of energy. Yet production GPU utilization averages just 50-60%, meaning organizations routinely waste 60-70% of their cloud compute budgets. Without real-time visibility, engineers only discover bottlenecks after hours of wasted spend. This talk shares lessons learned building a multi-layer profiling system in Go that captures metrics across application, system, and infrastructure layers with as little as 2-5% runtime overhead. We cover the architecture and design choices made, how they played out in production, and where Go’s concurrency primitives made the implementation practical at scale. The session walks through the three most costly performance failure modes we encountered: data pipeline bottlenecks (responsible for 40-50% of GPU underutilization cases), communication overhead in distributed training (accounting for 20-45% of total training time at scale), and straggler workers in heterogeneous environments (causing 30-40% of compute time to be wasted waiting for slower workers). For each, we explain what we tried, what worked, and what we would do differently. Beyond detection, the talk covers an automated recommendation layer that achieved 85% accuracy in root-cause identification and delivered an average 32% throughput improvement without requiring deep performance engineering expertise. The concrete outcomes: training costs reduced by 30-50%, energy consumption cut by 40-60%, and total training time reduced by 20-35% while maintaining convergence quality.
