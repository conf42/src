Designing Resilient Self-Service Search in Go: From Failure Modes to Intelligent Fallback Architectures

Video size:

Abstract

Self-service search is often the first line of customer support—but also one of the most failure-prone systems. Traffic spikes, partial shard failures, or cascading fan-out effects can silently degrade results, leading to empty responses and increased support escalations. This talk explores how to design a resilient search architecture in Go that guarantees meaningful results—even under failure conditions. Instead of treating fallback as an afterthought, we elevate it to a first-class reliability mechanism through a dedicated fallback index and intelligent routing strategies. You’ll learn how fan-out amplification can turn rare outliers into systemic failures, and how a layered fallback approach mitigates this risk. We’ll walk through a production-ready architecture, including distributed retrieval, a lightweight fallback index optimized for efficiency, and circuit breaker–driven traffic routing (closed, open, half-open) for graceful degradation. We’ll also cover bulkhead-based resource isolation to protect fallback capacity, practical strategies for avoiding zero-result queries, and how to balance recall and precision using query-log-driven synonym optimization. Finally, we’ll connect infrastructure signals—like tail latency and circuit breaker activity—with user-centric metrics such as reformulation and escalation rates, enabling early detection of relevance issues before they impact users at scale. Attendees will leave with actionable patterns and design principles to build robust, user-centric search systems that fail gracefully without compromising experience.
