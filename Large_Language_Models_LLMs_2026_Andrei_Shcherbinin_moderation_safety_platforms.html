Building an ML Automoderation System for LLM-Augmented Online Platforms

Video size:

Abstract

In this session, I’ll explain how Social Discovery Group built a production ML automoderation system for user-to-user messaging at scale. The talk covers the full workflow: collecting/aligning chat data with Trust & Safety policies, hybrid models (rules + MLLMs for grey areas), thresholding under extreme imbalance, and routing to reviewer queues with priority scoring. We’ll dive into: - Data prep: sampling rare violations, policy-aligned labels. - Model stack: lightweight classifiers for high-risk (toxicity, spam), MLLMs for nuanced cases (harassment, grooming). - Evaluation: imbalance-aware metrics (precision@low recall), error analysis loops with moderator feedback. - Production: A/B thresholds, monitoring drift, safe rollouts as abuse evolves. This system processes millions of messages daily, reducing reviewer load by 60% while catching 95%+ of violations. Attendees will get a blueprint for LLM-powered moderation pipelines that balance safety, scale, and false positives  critical for LLM-generated content and agentic interactions.
