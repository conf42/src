AI in Hiring: How Data Infrastructure Powers Smarter Talent Decisions

Video size:

Abstract

Enterprise talent acquisition runs on data infrastructure, and most of it is not built for the job. Traditional applicant tracking systems were designed for administrative workflow, not for the scale, speed, or analytical demands of modern hiring operations. This session shares what it looks like to move beyond that baseline and what the data architecture decisions look like in practice. We examine five progressive capability layers in enterprise recruiting systems, from legacy ATS deployments to self-optimizing talent pipelines, and walk through the infrastructure that makes each layer possible: skills graph frameworks, candidate matching models, vector databases, and the real-time data pipelines that feed predictive hiring workflows. These are not theoretical constructs. They are design choices with real trade-offs, and this session covers what worked, what did not, and what we would approach differently. A significant portion of the session focuses on governance and risk. Algorithmic screening tools are under active regulatory scrutiny and litigation, including cases involving major HR platforms. We cover how bias detection, model auditing, and human oversight were built into the system and why those decisions were made, not just what was deployed. Attendees will leave with a practical maturity framework for evaluating talent acquisition infrastructure, an understanding of the Five Layers of Predictive Talent Acquisition, and governance considerations they can apply when deploying AI in high-stakes automated decision-making.
