Beyond Dashboards: Deep-Dive Network Observability with eBPF

Video size:

Abstract

Why do dashboards show “green” while users experience failures? Because traditional monitoring tells you when a system is broken, but it rarely tells you why. In this session, I break down the paradigm shift from legacy monitoring to true observability, specifically focusing on the most elusive layer: the network. I’ll dive into the “black box” of Kubernetes networking, explaining why traditional iptables and sidecar-based approaches often struggle to capture the full picture. I’ll introduce eBPF as the game-changing alternative, demonstrating how it operates as a kernel-level superpower that bypasses legacy bottlenecks. You will learn how eBPF-based agents deployed as DaemonSets, can provide granular, real-time visibility into every packet traversing your cluster. Finally, I’ll walk through an open-source observability toolkit you can leverage today to map Pod-to-Pod and Pod-to-Node traffic. I’ll share a practical blueprint for building a network-aware platform that makes your infrastructure transparent, scalable, and most importantly easy to debug.
