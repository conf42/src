LLM Enabled Multi Document Correlation for Financial Compliance and Fraud Detection

Video size:

Abstract

Enterprise financial compliance is increasingly challenged by multi jurisdictional regulations, growing data volumes, and sophisticated fraud patterns that exploit inconsistencies across financial records. Traditional rule based systems and document level NLP approaches remain limited in detecting relationships that span multiple datasets. This session presents an AI driven multi document correlation framework enhanced with large language models to enable cross document reasoning and fraud detection. The approach combines three key components: a graph based entity correlation engine that connects payroll, tax, procurement, and transactional data; an adaptive probabilistic risk model that aggregates anomaly signals into calibrated risk scores; and a normalization layer that aligns currencies, tax structures, and reporting standards across jurisdictions. Large language models are incorporated to improve semantic understanding, contextual linking, and interpretation of unstructured financial documents. The framework was evaluated on approximately 3 million anonymized records across four jurisdictions over five years, reflecting real world enterprise conditions. Results demonstrate strong performance with high precision and recall, a significant reduction in false positives compared to rule based systems, and measurable decreases in manual audit workload. By enabling continuous cross document analysis and learning from audit feedback, this approach shifts compliance from a reactive validation process to a predictive and intelligence driven model. The session will highlight architectural design, evaluation insights, and practical considerations for applying large language models in scalable enterprise compliance systems.
