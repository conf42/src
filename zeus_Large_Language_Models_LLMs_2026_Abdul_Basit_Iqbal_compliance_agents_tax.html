Agentic AI for Autonomous Compliance in Enterprise Tax Systems Using LLM-Driven Tax-to-Code Pipelines

Video size:

Abstract

Modern enterprise tax systems operating across multinational jurisdictions face a growing challenge in translating continuously evolving legal regulations into executable compliance logic. Traditional compliance workflows rely heavily on manual interpretation of statutory text, resulting in update cycles that can take months and expose organizations to significant compliance drift and financial risk. This talk presents an LLM-driven Autonomous Compliance Infrastructure (ACI) that applies agentic AI principles to automate the end-to-end transformation of regulatory changes into deployable tax logic. The architecture combines Legal NLP, multi-agent orchestration, formal rule modeling, and a Tax-to-Code Engine that separates declarative regulatory policy from core application logic. The session focuses on practical system design decisions, explainability mechanisms, human-in-the-loop governance, and deployment validation strategies used to ensure reliability in legally critical environments. It also covers lessons learned while integrating large language models into enterprise-grade compliance workflows, including uncertainty handling, rollback strategies, observability, and scalability challenges. Attendees will gain insight into how agentic LLM systems can reduce compliance update cycles from months to hours while maintaining governance, auditability, and operational trust in real-world enterprise platforms.
