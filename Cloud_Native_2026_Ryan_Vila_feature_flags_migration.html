Beyond the Toggle: Feature Flags as Your Cloud-Native Migration Safety Net

Video size:

Abstract

Cloud migrations, API rewrites, and architecture transformations don’t usually fail because of bad code; they fail because teams rely on “big bang” releases that concentrate risk into a single moment.

More than half of large-scale transformations never fully deliver on their intended outcomes. The difference between failure and success isn’t the migration itself; it’s how you release it.

In this talk, we’ll explore how modern engineering teams use feature flags and experimentation as a cloud-native safety net, decoupling deployment from release to enable gradual, reversible, and data-driven migrations.

You’ll learn how to:

  • Replace high-risk cutovers with controlled, observable progressive rollouts
  • Measure real outcomes such as performance, cost, and reliability, not just deployment success
  • Safely test in production with guardrails, governance, and automated rollback
  • Modernize systems incrementally, retiring legacy components and validating new services without disruption

Whether you’re replatforming to the cloud, rebuilding APIs, or evolving a monolith into microservices, this session will give you a practical roadmap to reduce risk while accelerating delivery.
