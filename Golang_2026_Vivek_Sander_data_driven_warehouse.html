Data-Driven Warehouse Design for Scalable, Risk-Resilient 3PL RFPs

Video size:

Abstract

Large-scale 3PL RFPs demand more than competitive pricing—they require data-backed operational credibility. Yet many proposals still rely on generic assumptions, creating misalignment with real-world operations and increasing implementation risk. This session presents a data-driven warehouse solution design framework that transforms raw logistics data into accurate, scalable, and implementation-ready proposals. The approach begins with throughput analysis, using inbound metrics such as containers received, pallets handled, and peak variability, alongside outbound indicators like order volume, order complexity, and shipment patterns to define true capacity requirements. It then extends to order profile analysis, where SKU breadth, order size distribution, and pick types inform the selection of optimal fulfillment strategies such as batch, zone, or wave picking. A key component is SKU and inventory profiling through velocity-based segmentation to optimize storage placement, reduce picker travel time, and improve throughput efficiency. These insights guide warehouse layout and storage design, aligning material flow, dock configuration, and storage systems with actual operational constraints. The session also covers labor modeling, translating operational volumes into workforce requirements using engineering standards to ensure accurate planning in labor-intensive environments. Equipment and facility investments are derived directly from these models, enabling transparent and defensible cost structures. Finally, the framework emphasizes customer validation and data continuity, ensuring alignment before submission and a smooth transition from proposal to implementation.
