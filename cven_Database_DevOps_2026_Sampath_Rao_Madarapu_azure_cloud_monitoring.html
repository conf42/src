AI Assisted Azure Ops with Copilot for Diagnostics and Resource Control

Video size:

Abstract

Artificial Intelligence is reshaping cloud and data platform operations by combining human expertise with intelligent automation. Copilot in Azure reflects this shift by introducing an AI powered assistant that improves resource management and diagnostic insight across complex Azure environments. Modern cloud platforms support hundreds of services and thousands of resource types. For DevOps and database teams, managing configurations, monitoring performance, and diagnosing issues often requires deep expertise and constant context switching. Copilot simplifies this experience through natural language interaction, allowing users to explore their environments conversationally. Teams can summarize configurations, investigate performance metrics, and understand relationships between services and resources without navigating multiple tools. Behind this capability is an architecture that combines Large Language Models with Azure control plane data, configuration metadata, and real time telemetry. This integration enables Copilot to generate context aware insights that support faster diagnostics, operational visibility, and more efficient resource management across cloud workloads, including data and database services. The session will also highlight the role of engineers and technical advisors who contribute to Copilot’s diagnostic intelligence by validating telemetry mappings, reviewing system behavior, and building trusted troubleshooting guidance. Their input ensures that AI driven recommendations remain accurate, transparent, and operationally reliable. Attendees will gain insight into how AI assisted operations can support DevOps and database teams by improving troubleshooting workflows, strengthening observability, and enabling more responsive cloud governance in Azure environments.
