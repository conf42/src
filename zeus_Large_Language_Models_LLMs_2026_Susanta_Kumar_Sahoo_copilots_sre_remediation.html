LLM Copilots for SRE: Transforming Incident Response and Auto-Remediation

Video size:

Abstract

As digital systems grow increasingly complex, Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) teams must respond to incidents faster and with greater context than ever before. Large Language Models (LLMs) are emerging as powerful copilots that help teams detect, understand, and remediate production issues in real time. This talk explores how LLM-powered copilots are transforming incident management workflows. Modern AIOps platforms ingest massive volumes of telemetrymetrics, logs, and tracesand apply techniques such as anomaly detection, clustering, and change-point analysis. When paired with LLMs, these signals can be summarized, correlated, and translated into actionable insights for engineers during high-pressure incidents. Through a real-world case study, we examine an LLM-enabled chatbot integrated with PagerDuty that reduced mean time to detect (MTTD) by 31% by summarizing alerts, querying observability dashboards, and correlating incidents with recent code deployments. Beyond detection, LLMs are also augmenting auto-remediation systems. When combined with orchestration tools like StackStorm and Rundeck, LLMs enable context-aware remediation decisionssuch as traffic rerouting or automated rollbacksresulting in up to 42% fewer repeat incidents. However, reliability risks remain. LLM hallucinations, automation failures, and governance challenges make human oversight essential. This session discusses practical guardrails, human-in-the-loop workflows, and governance patterns that allow organizations to safely deploy LLM copilots in reliability-critical environments. Attendees will gain architectural insights and practical strategies for integrating LLM copilots into incident response systems, enabling faster detection, smarter remediation, and more resilient operations.
