Responsible Agentic AI for Equitable and Sustainable Cloud Native Telecom

Video size:

Abstract

Cloud native architectures are redefining telecommunications as 5G evolves toward 6G. Containerized network functions, distributed edge workloads, and AI driven automation now manage spectrum allocation, fault detection, traffic forecasting, and power optimization in real time. As networks become increasingly autonomous and software defined, the responsibility embedded within these intelligent systems becomes a critical cloud native concern. Globally, 4.0 billion people have high speed connectivity, yet 1.5 billion remain under connected and 1.1 billion remain completely unconnected. AI based resource allocation systems, when trained on historically uneven investment patterns, risk reinforcing disparities by prioritizing high revenue urban regions while underserved rural and economically disadvantaged areas receive limited infrastructure upgrades. Within cloud native orchestration layers, such bias can scale rapidly across distributed environments if fairness is not explicitly designed into automation workflows. Environmental impact adds another layer of complexity. Reinforcement learning can dynamically adjust base station power usage to reduce idle energy consumption, and digital twin simulations allow operators to test efficiency strategies before deployment. However, large scale model training and continuous inference across edge and cloud infrastructure require substantial computational resources. The sustainability outcome depends on whether optimization gains exceed the operational footprint of AI driven cloud workloads. This session presents an integrated governance framework that embeds transparency, accountability, fairness evaluation, and carbon reporting directly into AI enabled cloud native network operations. By combining audit mechanisms, allocation assessments, responsibility mapping, and measurable sustainability tracking, telecom providers can align autonomous optimization with equitable access and environmental responsibility. As cloud native networks become increasingly agent driven, responsible AI is essential to ensure scalable innovation delivers measurable societal value alongside performance.
