LLM-Driven Financial Ledgers: Building Intelligent, Real-Time Accounting Systems at Scale

Video size:

Abstract

The rise of Large Language Models (LLMs) is reshaping how financial systems are designed, built, and operatedespecially in traditionally rigid domains like General Ledger (GL) systems. This talk presents a practical, architecture-first approach to integrating LLMs into AI-powered financial ledgers, drawing from real-world system design and deployment experiences in the insurance sector. We explore how LLMs augment event-driven architectures built on Apache Kafka by enabling intelligent processing of unstructured financial data, automating journal entry generation, and enhancing reconciliation workflows through contextual understanding. Beyond theory, this session dives into what actually works in production: combining LLMs with structured ML models (such as LSTM and Random Forests), implementing retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for auditability, and addressing latency, cost, and reliability challenges in high-throughput environments. We also discuss lessons learnedfailure modes, prompt engineering pitfalls, and observability strategies that made complex AI systems manageable. Attendees will gain actionable insights into designing scalable, compliant, and efficient LLM-driven financial platforms, including security considerations and performance tuning. This talk is ideal for engineers and architects looking to move beyond experimentation and build robust, real-world LLM applications in fintech.
