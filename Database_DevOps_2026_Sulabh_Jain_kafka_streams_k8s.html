Cloud-Native Core Systems: Database & DevOps for Insurance

Video size:

Abstract

Insurance organizations face growing pressure to modernize legacy core platforms as regulatory demands increase and digital competitors accelerate delivery cycles. Decades-old monolithic systems—particularly tightly coupled application and database layers—limit agility, slow releases, and struggle to scale during catastrophe-driven claims spikes. This session examines how cloud-native engineering, combined with modern database and DevOps practices, transforms core insurance systems. We explore microservices architectures that decouple data domains, containerization and Kubernetes orchestration for high availability, and Infrastructure-as-Code for reproducible, resilient database and application environments. CI/CD automation enables frequent, reliable deployments backed by automated functional, performance, and security testing. Event-driven architectures using platforms such as Kafka or EventBridge improve resilience and reduce cascading failures, while API-first design supports secure, versioned data exchange across partner ecosystems. Modern observability—centralized logging, distributed tracing, and metrics—enables teams to diagnose incidents across distributed systems containing hundreds of services and data stores. The session also addresses operational challenges including distributed data consistency, schema evolution, and organizational change, outlining approaches such as service mesh adoption, upskilling programs, blameless postmortems, and sustained executive commitment. Attendees will gain a research-backed roadmap for modernizing legacy insurance platforms into scalable, resilient, DevOps-driven cloud-native systems.
