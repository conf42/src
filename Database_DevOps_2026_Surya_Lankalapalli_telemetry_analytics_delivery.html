Keeping Databases Always Available: Active-Active DNS Strategies for DevOps and Failover

Video size:

Abstract

Database availability is critical for modern applications, yet DNS is often an overlooked dependency that can delay failover and disrupt connectivity during outages. Traditional single-provider and failover-based DNS models introduce latency, stale records, and operational complexity that directly impact database resilience. This talk explores how an Active-Active, multi-provider DNS architecture can improve database availability in DevOps-driven environments. By running multiple DNS providers simultaneously, systems can route traffic in real time based on health checks and endpoint availability ensuring faster failover for primary/replica databases and minimizing downtime. We’ll examine how DNS integrates with database replication, failover automation, and global read/write routing strategies. Attendees will learn practical approaches to synchronizing configurations, reducing recovery time, and improving observability across distributed systems. This session is ideal for engineers managing databases at scale who want to strengthen reliability, optimize failover, and align DNS strategy with modern DevOps practices.
