AI-Driven Database DevOps: From Predictive Tuning to Autonomous Operations in Production

Video size:

Abstract

Modern database systems are at the heart of DevOps-driven architectures, yet performance tuning, scaling, and reliability management remain highly manual and reactive in many organizations. In this talk, we share practical lessons from implementing AI-driven database optimization techniques in real-world production environments, focusing on what actually works—and what doesn’t—when integrating intelligence into database DevOps workflows. We explore how machine learning models can be embedded into CI/CD and operational pipelines to enable predictive workload forecasting, allowing teams to proactively allocate resources and avoid performance bottlenecks during peak demand. Through concrete examples, we demonstrate how automated indexing strategies—powered by reinforcement learning—improved query performance for analytical workloads while reducing storage overhead compared to traditional approaches. The session also dives into AI-based anomaly detection integrated with observability stacks, highlighting how teams reduced incident detection time from minutes to seconds and significantly lowered false alerts. We discuss how these improvements translated into faster debugging, more reliable deployments, and reduced operational toil. Beyond the successes, we provide an honest look at challenges encountered, including data quality issues, integration with legacy systems, and the effort required to operationalize AI models within existing DevOps toolchains. We outline the trade-offs between automation and control, and what we would approach differently in future implementations. Finally, we present a practical roadmap toward semi-autonomous database operations, where feedback loops continuously optimize performance with minimal human intervention. Attendees will leave with actionable insights, architecture patterns, and tooling strategies they can apply to improve database performance, scalability, and reliability in their own DevOps environments.
