Debugging debugging; why we must approach debugging differently

Video size:

Abstract

We will delve into the reasons why debugging techniques are broken. Why a method that was developed last century is no longer relevant and what advances have been made in the space to ensure that everyone can use the right tools for the right use case

Summary

  • How do we debug debugging? Why do we need to approach it differently in order to work more effectively? How do we empower teams to develop the highest quality code that they can in the quickest amount of time?
  • A study found that 75% of developers time was spent on solving bugs. Our engineers often get less satisfaction from fixing broken things than creating new, shiny features. The tools we give our teams are simply not right for the job at hand. How can we focus on the positive in order to address some of the issues with debugging?
  • Give your teams a cloud native toolbox. Train the team share use cases. Data is gold, but too much will weigh you down. Make sure it's easily accessible and usable.
