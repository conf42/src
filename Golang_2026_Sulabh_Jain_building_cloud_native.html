Building Cloud-Native Insurance Core Systems with Go

Video size:

Abstract

Insurance organizations are under mounting pressure to modernize legacy core platforms as customer expectations rise, regulatory demands increase, and digital-native competitors accelerate innovation. Decades-old monolithic systems built for stability now limit agility, require lengthy release cycles, and struggle to handle demand surges such as catastrophe-driven claims spikes. This session examines how cloud-native engineering—implemented with Go-based microservices—enables resilient, high-velocity core systems. We explore service decomposition, containerization, and Kubernetes orchestration to support independent scaling of high-volume capabilities like quote generation and claims intake. Kubernetes enhances availability through automated deployment, scaling, self-healing, and progressive delivery strategies including canary and blue-green releases. Infrastructure-as-code improves repeatability and disaster recovery by enabling complete environment reconstruction within hours rather than weeks. CI/CD automation compresses release cycles from quarterly deployments to multiple releases per day, supported by automated functional, performance, and security testing. Event-driven architectures using platforms such as Kafka or EventBridge increase resilience by reducing cascading failures and supporting eventual consistency. API-first design enables secure partner integrations through documented interfaces, centralized authentication, and versioned contracts. Modern observability—centralized logging, distributed tracing, and metrics—provides visibility across ecosystems of distributed services. The session also addresses challenges including distributed data consistency, operational complexity, and cultural change, outlining practical solutions grounded in the research. Attendees will gain a research-backed roadmap for transforming legacy insurance platforms into agile, scalable cloud-native systems using Go.
