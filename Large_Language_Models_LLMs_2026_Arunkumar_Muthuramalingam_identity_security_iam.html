Identity-Centric Security for AI Systems: IAM as Cybersecurity Infrastructure

Video size:

Abstract

The rapid adoption of large language models (LLMs), AI copilots, autonomous agents, APIs, and cloud-native automation has fundamentally expanded the identity attack surface. Modern AI systems rely on complex interactions between users, services, models, plugins, orchestration frameworks, and machine identities, making traditional perimeter-based security increasingly ineffective. As enterprises integrate LLMs into production workflows, identity has emerged as the primary control plane for securing AI-driven infrastructure. This session explores identity-centric security as a foundational architecture for modern AI ecosystems, reframing identity and access management (IAM) from an administrative capability into critical cybersecurity infrastructure. Rather than focusing on individual tools, the talk examines identity as a structural control governing authentication, authorization, privilege boundaries, and trust relationships across distributed AI systems. The presentation highlights several infrastructure-level impacts. First, identity-driven least privilege reduces attack surfaces through granular, context-aware, and time-bound access controls for both human and non-human actors. Second, continuous identity verification limits lateral movement and privilege escalation across AI pipelines, APIs, vector databases, orchestration layers, and cloud workloads. Third, centralized identity governance improves resilience by enabling rapid containment, revocation, and recovery when AI systems are compromised. The session also addresses the growing dominance of non-human identities, including AI agents, automation services, and machine-to-machine workloads, which increasingly outnumber human users in modern environments. By positioning identity as the security boundary for AI ecosystems, this talk provides a strategic framework for building scalable, resilient, and trustworthy LLM-powered systems where identity, not the network perimeter, becomes the foundation of defense.
