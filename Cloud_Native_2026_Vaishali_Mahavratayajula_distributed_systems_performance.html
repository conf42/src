Security in Your Pocket: AI Risk Assessment for Cloud-Native SMBs

Video size:

Abstract

As small and home-based businesses adopt cloud-native platforms—e-commerce SaaS, APIs, serverless backends, cloud services, and social media integrations—their digital footprint becomes distributed, dynamic, and difficult to secure. Unlike enterprises, these organizations lack security teams and DevSecOps pipelines. Enterprise security platforms assume complex ownership models, while consumer tools overlook business-level risk. This creates a widening cloud-native security gap. This talk presents Security in Your Pocket, an AI-driven, self-service cybersecurity framework implemented through the securemystore.ai platform for SKRM Technologies. The framework addresses four systemic gaps: limited cloud asset visibility, absence of clear security ownership, uncontrolled third-party and AI tool exposure, and misalignment between traditional security solutions and small business realities. Modern small businesses operate across constantly changing cloud assets—websites, APIs, SaaS tools, e-commerce platforms, and cloud services—many of which fall outside traditional security monitoring. Our six-layer architecture integrates asset discovery, vulnerability detection, continuous monitoring, AI correlation and intelligence, business-aligned risk scoring, and continuous attestation. AI models correlate risks across distributed assets and translate technical findings into plain-language remediation guidance. Evaluation in simulated small business cloud environments identified misconfigurations, weak authentication, and exposed services with minimal false positives. Results show improved visibility, faster prioritization, and actionable protection without requiring dedicated security expertise. This session demonstrates how AI-enabled human–AI collaboration can operationalize continuous cloud-native security as an accessible service—bringing automated risk intelligence directly into the hands of small digital businesses.
