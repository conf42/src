Iterative Threat Modelling: Enhancing Security in Agile Development

Video size:

Abstract

Injecting Threat Modeling into the agile software development lifecycle brings an adaptive approach for assessing and mitigating security threats incrementally. By regularly revisiting and refining the threat model, development teams ensure robustness of the application’s security remain up-to-date.

Summary

  • Jagdesh Chant: Threat modeling is an exercise used to identify threats or ways to attack a system. He says traditional threat modeling does not involve development teams. Chant outlines five steps to achieve agile threat modeling.
  • Scoping is an important step of threat modeling. We should be very conscious in including the right scoping of the exercise. Always focus on few chunks before performing the activity. For our simulation we will scope the customer login flow as our feature.
  • Evil brainstorming is where we wear attackers hat in coming up with ways to attack, break or frustrate a particular bit of software. The key principle is to be aware of the time and never go into rabbit hole discussion. There are multiple ways or methodologies to implement the evil brainstorming.
  • Using the threats model and security objective, we prioritize the cards. DDoS is prioritized top because it affects the availability and damages the reputation of the application. After identifying the threat, we are going to mitigate it. Feedback and continuous improvement is essential to managing risk.
