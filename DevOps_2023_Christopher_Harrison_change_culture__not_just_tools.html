How to change culture, not just tools

Video size:

Abstract

Just as people buy treadmills thinking it’ll make them runners, organizations buy tools assuming it’ll change their practices and behaviors. Spoiler alert: it doesn’t. The right tools are important, but only if there’s a shift in culture. Let’s explore how we can bring change from within. Having talked with many customers about their struggles to integrate and update their DevOps approach, one word stood out in every conversation: culture. Teams invested in new tools, put forth new processes, only to see them collect dust within weeks. The chief problem is may people weren’t buying in. As it turns out, new tools doesn’t mean new culture. The goal of this session is to talk through strategies for driving changes in culture. We’ll explore the keys to success for driving new culture, between identifying the right questions, finding the right persons, and driving the right behavior.

Summary

  • Buying a treadmill doesn't make you a runner. Having the right tool does in fact make a difference. But the tool itself is only going to be helpful if it's being used. Instead of focusing solely on what some cool new tool can do for us, let's focus on how we can actually use those tools.
  • The real question is why? What is it that we are bring to achieve? Our real goal is to be more responsive to customer requests. We also need to identify who is key to success. If we can get everybody on board, the rest doesn't matter.
  • One big strategy that you can use is to start to form new teams. Diverse teams are inherently going to be more successful. If we're already making a big change, it's the perfect time to now start to implement additional changes.
  • Change, particularly when we're talking about cultural change, is bring to take time and there are going to be setbacks. What's going to bring about the change is defining those goals, defining the why, finding the right people to champion that.
