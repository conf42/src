Cloud-Native Intent Orchestration for Enterprise AI Ecosystems

Video size:

Abstract

As enterprises embed generative AI into cloud-native platforms, they often face fragmentation—isolated copilots, duplicated capabilities, inconsistent experiences, and governance gaps across distributed systems. This talk presents intent-based orchestration as a cloud-native architectural pattern for unifying enterprise AI into a scalable, composable assistant ecosystem. At the center is an enterprise Agentic AI platform designed with cloud-native principles: modular services, API-driven integration, and elastic scalability. Core components include intent analyzers for interpreting requests, orchestration layers for intelligent task routing, and reasoning engines for context-aware execution. Supporting capabilities such as vector-based knowledge retrieval, dynamic prompt libraries, and state tracking enable reliable, high-quality outputs across environments. The session explores how organizations evolve from passive Q&A bots to agentic workflows capable of multi-step task execution using LLM function calling, prompt chaining, and distributed state management. Real-world examples illustrate cross-domain automation in finance, HR, and IT—while maintaining governance, compliance, and operational observability. Attendees will gain practical guidance on phased cloud-native implementations, integrating AI into microservices architectures, and establishing governance frameworks that align with DevOps and platform engineering practices. We will also examine emerging patterns in AI interoperability and collaborative agent networks. By the end, participants will understand how to architect resilient, interoperable, and scalable AI ecosystems that move beyond isolated tools—transforming enterprise AI into a unified, cloud-native capability.
