Engineering High-Performance Real-Time Leaderboards: From O(N²) to O(log N)

Video size:

Abstract

In this technical session, Ivan Balashov explores how to design and implement high-performance leaderboards capable of handling 100K+ concurrent users with real-time updates. He presents a case study where a production system suffered from daily outages due to O(N²) update complexity, CPU spikes, and memory pressure. The talk covers the end-to-end solution: an indexed skip list augmented with span counters for O(log N) position queries, combined with a hash-based user deduplication system. Ivan shares practical lessons from replacing a naive slice-based leaderboard with a memory-efficient, concurrency-safe, in-process solution delivered in just 2 days. Attendees will gain insight into probabilistic data structures, cache-aware design, and how to pragmatically balance technical excellence with urgent business requirements in production-critical systems.
