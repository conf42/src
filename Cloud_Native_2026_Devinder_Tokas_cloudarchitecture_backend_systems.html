Interoperability by Design for Cloud-Native Workflows

Video size:

Abstract

Cloud-native systems now span containers, Kubernetes clusters, data pipelines, microservices, and multi-cloud infrastructure. With organizations running an average of 2,341 containers and Kubernetes in production for 80% of enterprises, avoiding vendor lock-in and operational fragmentation is no longer optional it is architectural strategy. This session presents a five-plane interoperability framework purpose-built for cloud-native environments: Workflow Definition; Execution & State; Integration; Observability & Lineage; and Packaging & Deployment. The model is grounded in open standards central to the CNCF ecosystem, including CloudEvents, OpenTelemetry, OpenLineage, the OCI Image Specification, and Kubernetes CustomResourceDefinitions. We will review benchmark data demonstrating event-driven systems exceeding 200,000 events per second with median latencies as low as 32 ms in scale-out configurations. OpenTelemetry Collectors have been benchmarked at 15,000 events per second with under 12 ms query latency using 256 MB memory and 15 CPU. Beyond performance, we connect interoperability to measurable reliability outcomes: faster MTTR with mature observability practices, SLO-aware autoscaling that reduces violation duration and cost, and elite DevOps metrics including sub-5% change failure rates and recovery under 60 minutes. Attendees will leave with a contract-first architectural blueprint for decomposing monolithic workflow engines into portable, pluggable components enabling high throughput, low latency, standardized governance, and sustainable reliability at cloud-native scale.
